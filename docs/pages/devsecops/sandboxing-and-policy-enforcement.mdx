# Sandboxing & Policy Enforcement

<TagList tags={frontmatter.tags} />
<AttributionList contributors={frontmatter.contributors} />

Sandboxing alone does not solve security, but it is a necessary complement to **policy enforcement**. While policy controls determine whether an action should occur, sandboxing constrains what happens if an action is allowed.

Together, these mechanisms provide defense-in-depth by limiting the impact of mistakes, adversarial behavior, or unforeseen interactions between tools.

In production, sandboxing should be treated as an always-on control rather than a development-time feature, with continuous validation that isolation boundaries remain intact as systems evolve.
