cert:
- id: ms-1
  title: Governance & Inventory
  controls:
  - id: ms-1.1.1
    description: Is there a clearly named person or team accountable for multisig operations?
      Accountability scope must include policy maintenance, signer onboarding/offboarding
      coordination, documentation currency, periodic review scheduling, and incident escalation.
    title: Named Responsible Multisig Owner
  - id: ms-1.1.2
    description: Do you operate a documented maintenance process to keep multisig documentation
      current after any operational or signer change? Documentation must be updated within
      24 hours for security-critical changes (e.g., signer compromise, emergency key rotation)
      and within 3 days for routine changes (e.g., planned signer additions/removals, threshold
      adjustments).
    title: Multisig Documentation Maintenance Process
  - id: ms-1.2.1
    description: Do you keep an up-to-date registry of all multisigs? At minimum, the registry
      must include address, network, threshold, classification, purpose, signer addresses,
      controlled contracts, on-chain roles, and last review date.
    title: Current Multisig Registry Details
  - id: ms-1.2.2
    description: Do you maintain an up-to-date list of authorized signers and map them to the
      correct multisigs? The signer mapping must be reviewed at least quarterly with documented
      evidence of review.
    title: Authorized Signer Mapping Registry
- id: ms-2
  title: Risk Assessment & Management
  controls:
  - id: ms-2.1.1
    description: Do you define and maintain a formal classification system for multisig wallets
      that covers both impact factors (financial exposure, protocol criticality, reputational
      risk) and operational needs (response time requirements, coordination complexity)?
    title: Formal Multisig Classification System
  - id: ms-2.1.2
    description: Do you maintain documented criteria that map each classification level to
      required controls (thresholds, quorum composition, review cadence, etc)? Classification
      must include at least impact-based levels and operational types, with higher-risk
      classifications requiring stronger controls (more signers, higher thresholds).
    title: Classification Criteria and Controls
  - id: ms-2.1.3
    description: Do you periodically review and update classifications and associated controls
      when conditions change? Reviews must occur at least every 6 months, with triggered
      reviews when TVL changes significantly, new products launch, protocol upgrades occur,
      or security incidents happen. Document evidence of reviews.
    title: Review and Update Classifications
  - id: ms-2.1.4
    description: Do you maintain policies defining when timelocks, modules, and guards are
      required based on multisig classification, and what security review is needed before
      enabling any module or guard?
    title: Timelocks, Modules, and Guards Policies
  - id: ms-2.1.5
    description: Do you maintain a documented exception approval process for deviations from
      standard multisig policies? Exceptions must include an expiry date, documented compensating
      controls, a remediation plan to return to compliance, and higher-level approval for
      Critical-class exceptions.
    title: Exception Approval Process for Multisig
  - id: ms-2.1.6
    description: Do you maintain documented policies for segregating assets across multiple
      wallets based on value, operational needs, and risk tolerance (e.g., hot/cold separation,
      treasury distribution)?
    title: Wallet Segregation Policy
  - id: ms-2.1.7
    description: Do you maintain documented policies for contract-level security controls
      that complement multisig governance (e.g., address whitelisting, invariant enforcement,
      parameter bounds)?
    title: Contract-Level Access Controls Policy
- id: ms-3
  title: Signer Security & Access Control
  controls:
  - id: ms-3.1.1
    description: Do you maintain a documented process for cryptographic attestation of address
      ownership and signer affiliation for multisig signers? The process must include
      message signing with the intended signer address, verification via an independent tool,
      and documented proof of verification.
    title: Cryptographic Signer Identity Attestation
  - id: ms-3.1.2
    description: Do you maintain a documented standard for signer key management? Hardware
      wallets are required for all multisig operations, and signers must use dedicated
      signing addresses exclusively for multisig operations.
    title: Signer Key Management Standard
  - id: ms-3.1.3
    description: Do you maintain documented policies and procedures for securely backing up
      and protecting signer seed phrases and recovery materials?
    title: Signer Seed Backups and Protection
  - id: ms-3.1.4
    description: Do you maintain documented requirements ensuring seed phrases are disaster
      resistant (geographically distributed), theft resistant (no single point of compromise),
      and operator loss resistant (recoverable with absence of one operator)? Seed phrases
      must never be stored digitally, in cloud storage, or photographed.
    title: Seed Phrase Security Properties
  - id: ms-3.1.5
    description: Do you operate a documented lifecycle for adding, replacing, and removing
      signers, including offboarding and periodic access reviews? Offboarded signers must
      be removed within 48-72 hours for Emergency-class multisigs, 7 days for Critical-class,
      and 14 days for others. Access reviews must occur at least quarterly.
    title: Multisig Signer Lifecycle Management
  - id: ms-3.1.6
    description: Do you have a documented training and readiness program for signers before
      they are authorized to participate? Training must cover transaction verification
      procedures, emergency procedures, and security best practices. Annual refresher
      training is required, plus updates within 30 days of significant procedure changes.
      Training should include practical skills assessment.
    title: Signer Training and Readiness Program
  - id: ms-3.1.7
    description: Do you maintain documented standards for hardware wallet capabilities required
      for multisig signing (e.g., display requirements, clear signing support, PIN security
      features, PIN time-based lockouts, firmware integrity verification, and brute force
      protection mechanisms)?
    title: Hardware Wallet Standards
  - id: ms-3.1.8
    description: Do you maintain documented requirements for hardware wallet procurement through
      verified supply chains (direct from manufacturer or authorized resellers), including
      device authenticity verification procedures?
    title: Hardware Wallet Procurement and Authenticity
  - id: ms-3.1.9
    description: Do you require signers to use dedicated wallet addresses exclusively for
      multisig operations, with policies prohibiting use for other purposes?
    title: Dedicated Signing Addresses
  - id: ms-3.1.10
    description: Do you maintain documented standards for signer device security (e.g.,
      requirements for dedicated signing devices or network isolation for high-value operations)?
    title: Dedicated Signing Environment
  - id: ms-3.1.11
    description: Do you maintain documented requirements for signer diversity across organizational
      roles, external parties, and geographic distribution appropriate to the multisig classification?
    title: Signer Diversity Requirements
- id: ms-4
  title: Operational Procedures
  controls:
  - id: ms-4.1.1
    description: Do you maintain documented processes for transaction initiation, approval,
      simulation, execution, and confirmation, including who is authorized to initiate?
      Transaction simulation is required for all transactions.
    title: Documented Transaction Lifecycle Procedures
  - id: ms-4.1.2
    description: Do you maintain documented signing and verification procedures that must be
      followed before any signatures are applied? Verification must include at minimum
      chain ID, target address, calldata (decoded), value, nonce, and operation type.
      DELEGATECALL operations to untrusted addresses must be flagged as high risk.
    title: Signing and Verification Procedures
  - id: ms-4.1.3
    description: Do you maintain audit trails and retention for transaction reviews, approvals,
      execution, and post-execution confirmation? Audit trails must be retained for a minimum
      of 3 years and include proposer, approvers, verification evidence, timestamps, and
      any issues encountered.
    title: Audit Trails and Retention
  - id: ms-4.1.4
    description: Do you require each signer to independently verify transaction details through 
      a separate interface or data source before signing, rather than relying solely on the 
      initiator's representation?
    title: Independent Transaction Verification Requirement
  - id: ms-4.1.5
    description: Do you maintain a policy defining enhanced controls for high-risk transactions
      (emergency actions, large transfers, protocol configuration changes)? High-risk
      transactions require mandatory simulation, a waiting period where operationally
      feasible, and elevated threshold approval.
    title: Policy for High-Risk Transactions
  - id: ms-4.1.6
    description: Do you maintain documented standards for multisig technology and tools, and
      a formal evaluation process for adopting new ones? Evaluation should consider whether
      tools are open source or have been audited by at least 2 reputable firms, have no
      known critical unpatched vulnerabilities, and have established ecosystem adoption.
    title: Multisig Standards and Evaluation
  - id: ms-4.1.7
    description: Do you maintain documented backup infrastructure for multisig operations?
      Backup infrastructure must include an alternate signing UI, backup RPC providers
      (2-3 different services), and an alternate block explorer. Testing of backup
      infrastructure is combined with emergency drills per ms-6.1.4.
    title: Backup Infrastructure for Multisig
- id: ms-5
  title: Communication & Coordination
  controls:
  - id: ms-5.1.1
    description: Do you maintain dedicated primary and backup communication channels for multisig
      operations with documented membership controls and onboarding/offboarding procedures?
      Channels must use end-to-end encryption, require MFA for access, and use invitation-based
      membership with approval. Primary and backup channels must be on different platforms
      to avoid correlated failures.
    title: Multisig Primary and Backup Communications
  - id: ms-5.1.2
    description: Do you have procedures to verify the identity of signers during sensitive
      communications? Acceptable methods include pre-established code words, video call
      confirmation, or verification via a secondary authenticated channel. Evidence of
      procedure effectiveness can be demonstrated through drill records combined with ms-6.1.4.
    title: Signer Identity Verification Procedures
  - id: ms-5.1.3
    description: Do you maintain documented escalation policies that define response-time
      expectations, on-call coverage, and procedures for urgent coordination? Response
      times must align with operational classification - Emergency less than 2 hours,
      Time-Sensitive 2-12 hours, Routine 24-48 hours. Emergency-class multisigs must have
      documented 24/7 on-call rotation.
    title: Documented Escalation and On-Call Policies
  - id: ms-5.1.4
    description: Do you maintain procedures for responding to suspected communication channel
      compromise, including switching to backup channels and out-of-band verification, and ensure
      signers know how to invoke them? Procedures must be tested at least annually, which
      can be combined with emergency drills per ms-6.1.4.
    title: Channel Compromise Response and Verification
  - id: ms-5.1.5
    description: Do you maintain and distribute an up-to-date emergency contact list for multisig
      operations? The list must be reviewed every 6 months with documented confirmation that
      contacts remain valid. The list must include protocol security team, external security
      resources, legal/compliance contacts, and backup contacts for reaching signers in
      emergencies.
    title: Emergency Contacts for Multisig
- id: ms-6
  title: Emergency Operations
  controls:
  - id: ms-6.1.1
    description: Do you maintain written emergency playbooks covering key compromise, lost
      access, communication channel compromise, and urgent protocol actions? Each scenario
      must have step-by-step procedures and escalation paths.
    title: Emergency Playbooks for Compromise
  - id: ms-6.1.2
    description: For critical/emergency-class multisigs, do you provide 24/7 paging to reach
      the required threshold and document escalation paths?
    title: 24/7 Paging for Emergency Multisigs
  - id: ms-6.1.3
    description: Do you maintain monitoring infrastructure and procedures to detect unauthorized,
      anomalous, or suspicious activity across all multisigs, with documented alerting and
      escalation paths? Monitoring must detect at minimum signer/threshold changes, transfers
      exceeding defined thresholds, nonce gaps, interactions with previously unknown addresses,
      failed transactions, and module/guard changes. Monitoring infrastructure must be
      protected against tampering.
    title: Multisig Monitoring and Alerts
  - id: ms-6.1.4
    description: Do you conduct periodic rehearsals and drills of emergency playbooks to test
      response procedures, communication channels, and signer coordination under simulated
      emergency conditions? Drills must occur at minimum annually, or bi-annually for
      Emergency-class multisigs, and after major procedure changes. Document drill date,
      participants, response times achieved, issues identified, and improvements made.
      These drills can incorporate backup infrastructure testing (ms-4.1.7), channel
      compromise response (ms-5.1.4), and identity verification procedures (ms-5.1.2).
    title: Rehearsals for Emergency Playbooks

# SFC - Multisig Operations

The SEAL Framework Checklist (SFC) for Multisig Operations provides best practices for managing multisig wallets securely.  It covers governance, risk management, signer security, operational procedures, and emergency operations.

For more details on certifications or self-assessments, refer to the [Certification Guidelines](/certs/certification-guidelines).
