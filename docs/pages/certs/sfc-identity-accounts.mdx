      - Recovery email addresses use organizational domains, not personal email

      - Backup and recovery codes stored in the password manager or comparable

    title: Recovery Methods Restricted to Organizational Channels

      - Account creation requires documented approval and is provisioned with

      - Offboarding revokes access across all organizational accounts within 24

# SFC - Identity & Accounts

The SEAL Framework Checklist (SFC) for Identity & Accounts provides guidelines for managing organizational accounts (social media, email, SSO, registrar, SaaS, privileged platforms) such that account compromise cannot redirect users, funds, or trust.

**Scope**: This is a horizontal cert covering the cross-cutting account-management pattern that applies to multiple surfaces (domains, code, custody, social media). Other certs reference it for account-control concerns while retaining their domain-specific substance (e.g., [SFC - DNS Registrar](/certs/sfc-dns-registrar) handles DNSSEC and registry locks; this cert handles registrar account hygiene).

**Related certs**:

- [SFC - DNS Registrar](/certs/sfc-dns-registrar) — DNS and registrar technical controls
- [SFC - DevOps & Infrastructure](/certs/sfc-devops-infrastructure) — repository, CI/CD, and cloud infrastructure
- [SFC - Treasury Operations](/certs/sfc-treasury-ops) — custody platform and transaction security
- [SFC - Incident Response](/certs/sfc-incident-response) — incident handling flow

For more details on certifications or self-assessments, refer to the [Certification Guidelines](/certs/certification-guidelines).
